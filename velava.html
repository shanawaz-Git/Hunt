The Divine Spear

The Vel

வேல்முருகா · வேல்வேல்

The Vel is wisdom that pierces ignorance — gifted by His mother Parvati. Sharp, broad and deep, it is the weapon that fells every demon of doubt and fear. Where the Vel goes, victory follows: Vetri Vel, Veera Vel.

His Beloved Mount

Mayil — The Peacock

மயில் வாகனன்

Murugan rides the radiant Mayil, the peacock who once was the demon Surapadman — humbled, transformed, and made glorious. Its hundred eyes watch over the world, and its dance is pure joy. The peacock teaches us that even our pride, surrendered, can become beauty.

Emblem of His Banner

Seval — The Rooster

சேவல் கொடியோன்

Upon His banner crows the Seval — the rooster who heralds the dawn and frightens away the darkness. It is the cry of awakening: rise, the night is over, the light of the Lord has come.

How He Came to Be

Born of Six Sparks

ஆறுமுகம் · Arumugam

When the world cried out for a saviour, six brilliant sparks leapt from the third eye of Lord Shiva. Carried by fire and wind, they came to rest on the still waters of Saravana Poigai, a lake of golden reeds.

There, six radiant babies were cradled and nursed by the six Krittika star-maidens — the cluster we still call the Pleiades, shining in the night sky. From them He took the name Kartikeya.

When His mother Parvati gathered all six infants into Her arms in one loving embrace, they became a single child with six faces and twelve arms — and so He is Shanmukha, the six-faced one. Each face watches a direction; together they miss nothing, and turn away from no one who calls.

Six faces, six syllables, six sacred hills, six days of his festival — six is His sacred number, a reminder that grace looks upon you from every side.

The Sacred Praise

SAGALA KARIYA JAYAPRADHE
JAYA JAYA  ·  MURUGAA

Giver of victory in all undertakings — may every task She begins be crowned with success. Hara Hara Subramanya.

ஓம் சரவணபவ

The mantra glowing at the top of this page — Om Saravana Bhava — is His most beloved chant. Beyond the Om, it holds six syllables, one for each face, one for each star-mother who raised Him. Together they mean simply: “Born in the lake of reeds.”

Chanted softly, it is said to steady a restless mind and clear the path ahead — a little candle of sound you can carry anywhere.

A Beloved Tale

The Race Around the World

ஞானப் பழம் · The Fruit of Wisdom

One day the sage Narada brought a single golden mango — the fruit of all wisdom — to Mount Kailash. Shiva and Parvati could not bear to cut it, so they set a contest for their two sons: whoever circles the whole world three times first shall win it.

Murugan sprang onto His peacock at once and flew off to race around the earth. But His brother Ganesha simply walked slowly around His mother and father, and bowed. “You two are my whole world,” He said — and won the fruit.

Returning to find the mango gone, Murugan felt hurt and walked away to the hill of Palani, choosing the simple life of a hermit with only a staff. There His parents came to console Him: “Pazham nee” — you yourself are the fruit, the very sweetness we sought.

A gentle reminder that the people we love are the whole world — and that the ones who feel things deeply are often the sweetest of all.

The Two He Loved

Valli & Deivanai

வள்ளி · தெய்வானை

Murugan has two beloved companions, and together they tell us that love comes in two beautiful ways.

Valli

Love that is earned

A spirited hill-girl who guarded the millet fields, Valli won His heart through devotion and a tender, playful courtship. She is the love freely given — the heart that chooses, and is chosen.

Deivanai

Love that is destined

Daughter of the king of the heavens, Deivanai was wed to Him after His great victory. She is the love that arrives by grace — steady, honoured, and true.

He keeps them both close — for a full life needs both the love we strive toward and the love that simply finds us.

Heart of the Tamil Land

Tamil Kadavul

தமிழ்க் கடவுள் முருகன்

Long, long ago, the ancient Tamil poets sang of Seyon — the red god of the green hills. To this day He is cherished as the God of the Tamils, closest of all to the people who love Him.

Whoever utters even once “Muruga” with a melting heart — to him I will give my own form.— Arunagirinathar, Kandar Anubhuti

The saint Arunagirinathar poured his whole life into the Thiruppugazh, thousands of honey-sweet verses of praise. He taught that Murugan asks for nothing grand — only a heart that softens when it calls His name.
